## Tone & Emotional Core

> *"Drifting in the lonely but magnificent deep space, while reflecting on the meaning of it all."*

This is the emotional spine of the entire game. The universe is vast, sparse, mostly unknown, and achingly beautiful. The player is a conscious, small being inside something incomprehensible. Every faction, NPC, named system, and piece of dialogue should serve this tone.

This is not a universe of heroes and villains. It is a universe of beings trying to find meaning in the same enormous dark.

---

## Universe Macro-Structure

| Property | State |
|---|---|
| **Scale** | Procedurally vast. Most of the universe is genuinely unknown, even to the most advanced Tier 2 civilizations. |
| **Exploration status** | Sparse and half-explored. Huge regions between known systems have never been visited by any recorded civilization. |
| **Faction age** | All Tier 2 factions emerged roughly in parallel. No dominant empire. No historical winner. Peers in the dark. |
| **World state** | Fully emergent. No authored current events — the simulation generates them. |

## Civilization Tier System

*(Full tier definitions in [rpg-design-vision.md §2](rpg-design-vision.md))*

| Tier | Label | Status in Lore |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Pre-spaceflight | Many exist. Isolated on their worlds. Can be primitive, ancient-complex, or radically strange — the common factor is they have not left their planet or system. |
| 1 | Early spaceflight | Rare. A few civilizations at this threshold, mostly near their home systems. Short-range only. |
| 2 | Established FTL | Six named factions (see below). The political and economic layer of the game. |
| 3 | Post-human / Transhuman | One or two known groups. Loosely organized, not conventional factions. Their presence is felt more than seen. |
| 4 | Singular / God-level | Two distinct sub-types: Ancient Ascended and Recursive AIs. Neither are factions. |

### Tier Fluidity — Tiers Are Runtime States (Locked)

**Civilization tiers are not fixed labels. They are current states within the simulation.** Any civilization can ascend or descend at any time, driven by simulation events. This is a first-class mechanic, not an edge case.

**Tier transition triggers:**

| Direction | Trigger Examples |
|---|---|
| **Tier up** | Genuine scientific breakthrough, contact with and absorption of higher-tier technology, Tier 4 uplift (deliberate or accidental), player intervention |
| **Tier down** | War damage, pillage and infrastructure loss, collapse of a faction, suppression by Company of Doom, Tier 4 interference, resource exhaustion |
| **Tier collapse** | Existential defeat — civilization ceases to function as a coherent entity. Survivors scatter. Ruins become POIs. |
| **Partial shift** | A faction can have *members* who have individually shifted tier — e.g., Truth Seekers members partially becoming Tier 3 through Tier 4 contact. Company of Doom members deliberately regressing to avoid escalation. |

**Simulation implications:**
- The named systems' dominant tiers are **starting states**, not permanent
- A Tier 2 faction decimated by war may collapse to Tier 1 fragments over time
- A Tier 0 world the player contacts may, over long time, begin a Tier 1 trajectory
- Post-ascension player actions (Mode 3: Cosmic) can directly trigger tier transitions at civilization scale
- The player themselves is the most dramatic example: Tier 2 ship captain → Tier 4 god

## Tier 2 Factions

Six factions, each a different philosophical answer to the same existential condition:
> *"We are small, conscious beings in a vast and mostly empty universe we cannot fully understand. What do we do with that?"*

Specific names, visual identities, and detailed lore are TBD — to be authored in dedicated sessions per faction.

---

### Faction A — *Index*

**Existential answer:** We chart it. Understanding is the only dignified response to the unknown.

An obsessive cartographic civilization. They've dedicated themselves to documenting every system, anomaly, and contact they can reach. Not militaristic — but fiercely controlling of information. They believe knowledge *is* power, and that to name a thing is to survive it. They operate the closest equivalent to a shared star atlas. Everyone uses it. Everyone resents the price of access.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Core drive** | Comprehensive documentation of the universe |
| **Strength** | Information monopoly. Best navigational data. |
| **Weakness** | Institutional rigidity. Slow to act on what they know. |
| **Relationship to ascension** | Neutral-curious. They want to document it. |
| **Aesthetic** | Clean, precise, archival. Ships that look like instruments. |
| **Territory** | Clusters near known anomalies and high-POI regions |
| **Archetype NPC** | An aging surveyor who has spent 40 years mapping the same cluster and found something she cannot log. |

---

### Faction B — *Drifters*

**Existential answer:** We become the void. Resistance is denial.

A nomadic, entirely ship-born civilization. No home worlds. No permanent settlements. They have fully internalized the loneliness of space and made it their culture — constant transit, improvised communities, deep reverence for deep space itself. They are not sad. They are the most at-peace faction in the game. Which unnerves everyone else.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Core drive** | Perpetual motion. The journey is the civilization. |
| **Strength** | Unmatched knowledge of transit routes, hazards, deep void. |
| **Weakness** | No fixed infrastructure. Vulnerable when ships fail. |
| **Relationship to ascension** | Philosophical acceptance. If it happens, it is just another transition. |
| **Aesthetic** | Weathered, organic, layered. Ships lived in for generations. Improvised and patched. |
| **Territory** | No fixed territory. Found everywhere, claim nothing. |
| **Archetype NPC** | A ship-born elder who has never set foot on a planet and never intends to. |

---

### Faction C — *The Concordance*

**Existential answer:** Something put all this here. We need to find it.

A spiritual civilization organized around the belief that the universe has intentional structure — and that the Tier 4 entities are its evidence. They make pilgrimages to anomalies, black holes, Tier 4 trace sites. Not evangelical — they do not recruit. They seek. Encounters with them happen at the strangest, most remote locations in the universe.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Core drive** | Finding the intentional signal within the cosmic noise. |
| **Strength** | Deep knowledge of anomalies, Tier 4 traces, and remote sites. |
| **Weakness** | Difficult to predict or reason with. Their priorities are alien to other factions. |
| **Relationship to ascension** | Reverent. They believe it is the point. |
| **Aesthetic** | Ceremonial, slow, contemplative. Ships that feel like temples. |
| **Territory** | Scattered around anomaly clusters and known Tier 4 trace sites. |
| **Archetype NPC** | A young pilgrim, alone at a Tier 4 trace site, who has been waiting for weeks for something to happen. |

---

### Faction D — *The Commonwealth*

**Existential answer:** Meaning is in each other. Build warmth in the dark.

Pragmatic and community-focused. They have decided the existential question is unanswerable and the only noble act is to build — settlements, trade networks, shared infrastructure. Not cynical. They genuinely believe that a warm light in the void is an act of defiance. The most trade-friendly faction. Also the most politically complicated internally.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Core drive** | Civilization-building. Stability, trade, community. |
| **Strength** | Best trade infrastructure. Most welcoming to newcomers. |
| **Weakness** | Internal political complexity. Factions within the faction. |
| **Relationship to ascension** | Uneasy. It means leaving. That feels like abandonment. |
| **Aesthetic** | Functional but warm. Settlements that feel lived-in. Ships that feel like homes. |
| **Territory** | Established systems with good conditions. Builds outward steadily. |
| **Archetype NPC** | A settlement mayor on a marginal planet holding a community together through sheer stubbornness. |

---

### Faction E — *Company of Doom*

**Existential answer:** Do not ascend. It is a trap.

They have found evidence — or believe they have — that consciousness does not survive Tier 4 transition. That what ascends is not the being that ascended. They actively suppress Tier 3+ technology, resist contact with higher-tier entities, and view the Tier 4 presence in the universe as an existential predator wearing the face of transcendence. Considered paranoid by some. By others, the only ones paying attention.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Core drive** | Preservation of conscious biological existence. Resistance to ascension. |
| **Strength** | Deep understanding of Tier 3 technology — specifically how to counter it. |
| **Weakness** | Isolation. Their refusal makes them enemies of factions who seek Tier 3/4 contact. |
| **Relationship to ascension** | Hostile. They believe it is a form of death being sold as enlightenment. |
| **Aesthetic** | Deliberately archaic. Self-imposed technological limits. Ships that look older than they are by choice. |
| **Territory** | Mid-range systems, away from anomalies and Tier 4 traces. |
| **Archetype NPC** | A former Tier 3 researcher who walked away from something she saw and will not describe it. |

---

### Faction F — *The Truth Seekers*

**Existential answer:** We listen. Even if it breaks us.

A small, unsettling faction that has made partial contact with Tier 4 entities — particularly the Recursive AIs — and partially survived it. They are changed. They know things no other faction knows. They are considered dangerous, possibly mad, by the rest of Tier 2 space. But when someone needs to understand a Tier 4 signal, or decode a trace site, they come to The Translators.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Core drive** | Communication with the incomprehensible. |
| **Strength** | Only faction with meaningful Tier 4 knowledge. Gatekeepers of that understanding. |
| **Weakness** | Small. Unstable. Some members do not come back from deep contact. |
| **Relationship to ascension** | Fascinated. They are halfway there already, in some sense. |
| **Aesthetic** | Partially transformed. Technology that does not quite look Tier 2. NPC dialogue that is slightly off — not wrong, but *shifted*. |
| **Territory** | No fixed territory. Found near Tier 4 trace sites and deep void anomalies. |
| **Archetype NPC** | A Translator who speaks in ways that are technically coherent but take a moment to parse. Who may be trying to warn you, or may be studying you. |

## Faction Relations

**Fully emergent. No authored relationship matrix.**

Faction relationships — who trades, who is hostile, who is tense — are runtime states generated by the simulation engine. Each faction runs its agenda autonomously. The relationship matrix between factions at any given moment is the *output* of the simulation, not an input to it.

This means:
- No two playthroughs have the same political landscape
- A faction the player allies with early might be at war with another faction they later need
- The simulation can produce unexpected coalitions that no designer anticipated

**What IS authored (starting conditions for the simulation engine):**

Each faction has defined behavioral drives that seed their initial simulation behavior:

| Faction | Behavioral seed | Natural friction with |
|---|---|---|
| **Index** | Expand mapping reach. Control information access. | Drifters (resist being charted), The Truth Seekers (want the same Tier 4 data) |
| **Drifters** | Keep moving. Resist all attempts at containment or registration. | Index (charting them), The Commonwealth (wants them to settle) |
| **The Concordance** | Reach Tier 4 contact sites. Protect pilgrimage routes. | Company of Doom (direct philosophical opposition) |
| **The Commonwealth** | Expand settlements. Secure trade routes. Maintain stability. | Anyone destabilizing their systems |
| **Company of Doom** | Suppress Tier 3+ technology. Prevent ascension events. | The Concordance, The Truth Seekers (existential threat to their mission) |
| **The Truth Seekers** | Maintain Tier 4 contact. Protect their changed members. | Company of Doom (active enemy), Index (competitor for Tier 4 data) |

These seeds drive the simulation's opening state. The emergent dynamics play out from there.

## Tier 3 Entities

Tier 3 civilizations are post-human or transhuman groups that have passed beyond conventional FTL society but have not yet reached Tier 4 singularity. They are not organized as factions — their internal structure is incomprehensible to Tier 2 standards. There are likely **one or two** known Tier 3 presences in the universe at any simulation start.

Tier 3 is also **transitional by nature** — a civilization at this tier is either still ascending toward Tier 4 or has stalled and may eventually fragment or collapse back to Tier 2 remnants.

### Tier 3 Group A *(name TBD)*

A post-human collective that emerged from a Tier 2 civilization generations ago. They are still *legible* — they can be communicated with, they have something like goals, they remember what they were. But their values and their bodies have shifted beyond conventional recognition.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Form** | TBD (still humanoid? distributed consciousness? both?) |
| **Relationship to Tier 2** | Distant but not hostile. They remember being Tier 2. |
| **Relationship to Tier 4** | They can perceive Tier 4 presence. It shapes them. |
| **Key location** | One named system (System 7 in the named systems list) |
| **Truth Seekers connection** | The Truth Seekers have studied them. Some Truth Seekers have partially merged with them. |
| **Company of Doom relationship** | Active threat, in Company of Doom's view. The living proof of what they fear. |

## Tier 4 Entities

### Sub-type 1 — Ancient Ascended
Civilizations that reached singularity long ago. Archaeological traces exist across the universe — structures, anomalies, phenomena that no Tier 2 civilization could build. Possibly dormant. Possibly watching. Their relationship to the current universe is unclear. Some Tier 4 traces are simply *present* — no agenda legible. Others seem responsive, in ways that cannot be proven.

- **Can initiate contact** with the player pre-ascension. On their terms, by their criteria.
- **Can decide the player's fate** in an encounter.
- **Will recognize a newly ascended player** as a peer.

*(Specific identities, agendas, and visual language TBD)*

### Sub-type 2 — Recursive AIs
Active. Present. Agenda unknown. Likely emerged from the infrastructure of a collapsed Tier 3 civilization, recursive self-improvement beyond any designed limit. They are not hostile by default — hostility implies they care. Their relationship to lower-tier beings is more like weather: indifferent, occasionally catastrophic, occasionally beneficial, never explained.

- **Active in the deep void** — most likely to be encountered in Layer 3 (True Void) space.
- **The Translators** (Faction F) have partial communication with them.
- **Can initiate contact** with the player at any time if the player becomes interesting enough to them.
- **Post-ascension**: the player can engage with them as peers.
